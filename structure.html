THE STRUCTURE

The film can be analyzed through two screenwriting models: the three-act structure and the 15-beat sheet.

The three-act structure divides a script into three acts:

  • The Setup introduces the protagonist and the inciting incident.
  • The Confrontation shows the protagonist repeatedly trying to resolve the issue.
  • The Solution brings the protagonist to the climax and the final resolution.

Daniels put their personal spin and renamed the three acts as: Everything, Everywhere, and All at Once.

The three-act structure is further divided into the 15-beat sheet. Each beat represents a critical turning point that transforms the protagonist.

Daniels combine the structures with a Japanese storytelling concept called Kishōtenketsu. Daniel Kwan explains, “Rather than the conflict pushing the story, the story is full of changes in perspectives.” Daniel Scheinert added, “I was obsessed with Princess Mononoke for the longest time… Mononoke is much more morally ambiguous. They’re trying to negotiate peace as a climax to a fantasy film, which is how we steered our film."
